About this guide

"A girl who was trying to figure out life and love and relationships and started asking a shit ton of questions. The more questions I asked, the more questions I got. Everybody had a different version, and every community had a different version — and so hence this lexicon was born."

You ever think you're talking about the same thing and it turns out you're absolutely not?

Let's get on the same page — or at least try to. This is a guide nobody asked for but everybody needs. Good sex and good relationships require a shared operating language. This lexicon is a tool for building one.

This is not a glossary. It is a philosophical map that connects ancient wisdom traditions to present-day relational confusion to the questions that are coming whether we are ready for them or not. The method is genealogy as critique: tracing where concepts came from, exposing the assumptions baked into their origins, and using that excavation to imagine things differently.
